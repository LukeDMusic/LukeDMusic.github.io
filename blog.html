Interdisciplinary Music Teaching

          I recently began this semester's observation hours at a nearby elementary school. During my time there, I witnessed the third grade class doing a unit about taking a "world tour" around the world. Each student had small paper booklets that served as their passports, and each class they would visit a new location and add a sticker from the area of the day. As students would ask to go to the bathroom, the teacher would respond, "Hurry back so you don't miss our tour of Ireland!" This is not only a great way to present world music in an exciting way, but also a great way to accomplish interdisciplinary music teaching.

          The world music lesson began after they had warmed up with a beginning song. However, before they actually began to sing the location's song they first learned about the country's music, instruments, flag, food, history, location, and culture in general. This helps engage the students, and the teacher encouraged hearing about students' personal connections to the country of focus. Interdisciplinary music teaching is easiest to do a the "in" to a lesson, like a funnel - wide on top, small on bottom. Through starting wide, learners will become more invested because they have a reason to be invested in the learning. We have all heard people say in math class, "When will I ever need this?" Starting wide is a perfect way to explain when and why you might care.
